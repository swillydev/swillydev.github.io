Criminal Appeals

If you have been convicted of an offence in the Magistrates' Court, you have the right to appeal to the Crown Court within 21 days of the date of conviction. Appeals to the Crown Court are a complete re-hearing of the original trial with the same or other witnesses and evidence being given. The appeal will be heard by a Crown Court Judge and at least one magistrate.
